Ural State Academy of Architecture and Arts hosts first StartUp Design Week

Ural State Academy of Architecture and Arts organized and hosted the very first Ural International Forum Design from April 1 to April 3, 2015, as part of StartUp Design Week. The main goal of the event was to create a way to communicate between young design professionals and experts in the architecture and business industries. Attendees included representatives from large companies like D-link, Convex, Salt, Wacom, IC ARCHITECT, Schneider Electric and others. The panel of judges included teachers, potential investors, entrepreneurs and experts from the MBA academy. Two contestants won best report, one of which was Anna Prokhorova, who created "personal products for the diagnosis and prevention of depression." This project has stirred a sizable controversy about the topic. Many activities took place at the event, including a panel that discussed "Modern vectors of design and architecture," a lecture about "National policy in the field of design," as well as several presentations from design professionals and design students.

Ural Airlines modifies rewards program

As Ural Airlines' Wings customer loyalty program has grown, the company has found it necessary to re-number program cards. Previously, members were assigned a seven-digit number. Those numbers will now contain 10 digits; existing members should just add a "100" prefix to their seven-digit number. For example, card number 1234567 would become 1001234567. Current Wings members can verify new account numbers by logging on to www.uralairlines.com and clicking the dashboard online. Once logged into your profile, click the "membership cards" tab. Those interested in signing up for a Wings account can do so at the same website. Wings participants can accumulate bonus points that can be used for free tickets and upgrades.

URFU hosts annual natural history festival

Ural Federal University's (URFU) Institute of Natural Sciences recently hosted a festival event to celebrate the IV Traditional Festival of Earth and Universe. The theme of the event was "Lasers Everywhere" in honor of the 50th anniversary of the Nobel Prize going to A. M. Prokhorov, Charles Townes and N. G. Basov; their work in quantum electronics, amplifiers and oscillators significantly contributed to the invention of the laser. Scientists from URFU explained to students that lasers can be used in many industries, including biology, physics, medicine, chemistry and astronomy. Alexander Germanenko, the head of the department of computer physics, presented a half-hour lecture about popular science titled "What is a laser"? Students then had the opportunity to break into study groups and learn how to use lasers in biology, physics, medicine, chemistry and astronomy. They also took a quiz about lasers. The students were also required to create several proposals about applying lasers in their industries. These ideas were given to a board of jury members. The winner will be announced at the next festival. Founded in 1920 and located in Yekarerinburg, URFU is the oldest university in the Ural region. It has 5,640 academic staff members and 57,000 students.
